Carlos Pineda-Antunez is a PhD student at the Comparative Health Outcomes, Policy, and Economics (CHOICE) Institute at the University of Washington. He focuses on advancing public health policies through health economics and decision sciences. His research evaluates the feasibility and effectiveness of prevention interventions for HIV, maternal health, and colorectal cancer. He develops decision models to assess the impact of innovative health technologies and preventive screening programs. He is an active member of the colorectal cancer group within the Cancer Intervention and Surveillance Modeling Network (CISNET) consortium and the Society of Medical Decision Making (SMDM).

## Research Interests

- Health economics and decision sciences
- Cost-effectiveness analysis and early-phase HTA
- Simulation modeling and model calibration
- Causal inference and impact evaluation
- HIV, maternal health, and colorectal cancer policy

## Recent Positions

- 2024-2025: Health Economist, Division of Health Policy and Management, School of Public Health, University of Minnesota (Minneapolis, USA)
- 2024-2025: Health Economist, CHOICE Institute, School of Pharmacy, University of Washington (Seattle, USA)
- 2024 (Jun-Sep): Decision Modeler, Department of Health Policy, School of Medicine, Stanford University (California, USA)

## Education
