Carlos Pineda-Antunez, a PhD student at the University of Washington's CHOICE Institute, focuses on advancing public health policies through health economics and decision sciences. He holds a Master of Science in Health Economics and a Bachelor of Science in Biomedical Engineering. Carlos has collaborated on public health research projects at prestigious institutions like the National Institute of Public Health of Mexico, the University of Minnesota, Stanford University, the University of Washington, and Washington Research Funding. His research evaluates the feasibility and effectiveness of health prevention interventions for HIV, maternal health, and colorectal cancer. Currently, he develops health decision models to assess the impact of innovative health technologies and preventive screening programs. Carlos is also an active member of the colorectal cancer group within the Cancer Intervention and Surveillance Modeling Network (CISNET) consortium and the Society of Medical Decision Making (SMDM).
