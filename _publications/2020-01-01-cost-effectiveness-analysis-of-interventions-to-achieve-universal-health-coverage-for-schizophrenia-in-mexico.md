This paper evaluates which schizophrenia interventions offer the best value for helping Mexico advance toward universal health coverage. The analysis compares the costs and expected health gains of different treatment options so that policy makers can see which packages are likely to generate the greatest benefit within limited budgets. For a general audience, the importance of the study is that expanding mental health coverage is not only about adding services; it is also about choosing combinations of care that are affordable, scalable, and clinically meaningful. The results identify interventions that appear more efficient than others and therefore more attractive for public financing. In that sense, the paper provides a practical roadmap for prioritizing mental health services where needs are high but resources remain constrained.
