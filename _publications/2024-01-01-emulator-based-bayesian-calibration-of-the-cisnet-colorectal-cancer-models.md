This peer-reviewed article presents an emulator-based Bayesian framework for calibrating CISNET colorectal cancer models more efficiently. Simulation models are central to cancer policy analysis, but formal calibration can require extensive computing time because many parameter combinations must be tested against observed data. The paper shows how an emulator can stand in for the full model during much of that search process, substantially reducing computation while preserving strong model fit and uncertainty quantification. For a general audience, the practical meaning is that policy models can be updated and evaluated faster without abandoning rigor. The work makes advanced calibration more accessible to research teams and supports more timely analysis of screening, prevention, and treatment strategies when decision makers need evidence on complex cancer policies.
