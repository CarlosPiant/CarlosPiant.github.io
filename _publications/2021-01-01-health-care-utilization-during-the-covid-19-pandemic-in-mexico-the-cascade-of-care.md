This article analyzes how the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted health care use in Mexico by tracing drop-offs across a cascade of care. Instead of treating utilization as a single outcome, the paper shows where people were most likely to disengage or face barriers, from seeking care to receiving services. That approach helps clarify whether the main problem was reduced demand, restricted availability, delays, or breakdowns later in the care process. For a general audience, the study makes the pandemic's indirect effects easier to understand: health emergencies do not only cause illness directly, they also interrupt ordinary care for many other conditions. The findings identify important points of loss in the care pathway and help inform strategies for service recovery and system resilience after major shocks.
