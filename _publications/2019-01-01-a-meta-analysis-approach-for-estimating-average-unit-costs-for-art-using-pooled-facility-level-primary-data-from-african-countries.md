This paper develops a meta-analytic approach to estimate the average unit cost of antiretroviral therapy using pooled facility-level data from multiple African countries. The main challenge it addresses is that single-site cost studies are often noisy, difficult to compare, and not always representative for planning. By combining data across settings, the study produces more stable cost estimates while still preserving information about differences between countries and service environments. For a general audience, the value of the paper is straightforward: governments and donors need realistic estimates of what treatment costs in order to budget, expand programs, and compare options. The findings show that pooled evidence can improve planning and can serve as a stronger empirical base than relying on isolated studies from individual clinics or programs.
