This paper presents DESCIPHR, an open-source pipeline in R for building discrete-event simulation models of cancer interventions and population health. The goal is to make complex policy models more transparent, reproducible, and easier for other researchers to adapt. Instead of treating simulation as a black box, the framework organizes the workflow needed to represent disease progression, interventions, calibration, and uncertainty in a more systematic way. For a general audience, the contribution is that better modeling tools can improve how researchers compare screening, prevention, and treatment strategies before those decisions are implemented in the real world. The main finding is methodological: an open, well-structured simulation pipeline can support rigorous cancer policy analysis while lowering barriers to reuse, peer review, and collaborative model development.
