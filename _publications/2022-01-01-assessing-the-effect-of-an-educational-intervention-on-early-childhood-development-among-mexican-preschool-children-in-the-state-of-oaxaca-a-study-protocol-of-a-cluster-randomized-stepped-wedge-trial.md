This paper presents the protocol for a cluster-randomized stepped-wedge trial designed to evaluate an educational intervention for preschool children in Oaxaca, Mexico. As a protocol, its main purpose is to explain clearly how the study will be conducted, what outcomes will be measured, and why the design is appropriate for the setting. The stepped-wedge approach allows the intervention to be rolled out over time while still supporting rigorous evaluation. For a general audience, the value of the paper is transparency: it shows in advance how the research team plans to test whether the program improves child development. That helps readers judge the study's rigor and gives other researchers and practitioners a model for evaluating similar early childhood interventions in real-world contexts.
