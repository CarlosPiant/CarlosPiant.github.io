This preprint introduces an emulator-based Bayesian approach for calibrating CISNET colorectal cancer models, with the goal of making calibration faster and more computationally practical. Large simulation models are useful for policy analysis, but calibration can be very time-consuming because the model must be run repeatedly to match observed data. The paper shows how a statistical emulator can approximate the behavior of the full model and dramatically reduce the computing burden while still preserving the logic of Bayesian calibration. For a general audience, the contribution is methodological but important: faster calibration means researchers can update models, test assumptions, and evaluate policies more efficiently. The preprint demonstrates that this approach can reproduce observed patterns while making complex cancer modeling more scalable and reproducible.
