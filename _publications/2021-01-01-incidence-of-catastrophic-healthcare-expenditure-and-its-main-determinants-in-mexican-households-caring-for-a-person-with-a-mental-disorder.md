This paper estimates how often Mexican households face catastrophic health spending when caring for a person with a mental disorder and examines the factors associated with that risk. The issue is especially important because mental health conditions often require long-term treatment, repeated visits, medicines, and caregiving time, all of which can strain household finances. The study shows that financial hardship is not evenly distributed and is shaped by socioeconomic conditions and patterns of health care use. For non-specialists, the central message is that mental illness can generate economic vulnerability as well as clinical need. The paper highlights the importance of stronger financial protection and better access to affordable mental health services so that care does not push already vulnerable families into deeper hardship.
